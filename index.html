Coming To America

I came to the United States in 2015 to continue living with my partner Nika. We met 2 years earlier when she was studying in Australia. By this stage we had largely exhausted her visa options for staying on, and I was ready to try out her home place for a while. Being a musician I found Chicago hugely inspiring and energizing, and with the help of Nika's father; Gene, my immigration process was soon underway. Having grown up in the bureaucratic jungle-gym of former Soviet Uzbekistan, and being the first of his large family to make the move to the US; Gene was a highly accomplished immigration guide. Nika and I were soon wed, and life began in the States in earnest. In Australia I had made my living as a touring musician, but by this stage I was more interested in following a more creative path. I had just released my first record and got busy on the creation of the next. I found work in other areas, namely as a sound engineer and music teacher, but after some time found the work unsatisfying. Gig work is relentless. I was continually exhausted, and the sound work was beginning to damage my hearing. In weighing up different paths, coding seemed to make the most sense. It tickles my need for fastidious activity.

The Future

I'm excited to come along for the ride of how programming continues to shape and define the world we live in. It's exciting to know that I'll be a voice amongst that progression. I'm also looking forward to grounding. After all these years of the rambling musician life, I'm eager to begin growing a life of stability; to ground in the profession as I continue to learn and develop my skills; to ground in the States; and to ground in my life with my partner and community.
